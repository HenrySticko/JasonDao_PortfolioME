Objective: Speed & Stability

This project required the design and fabrication of a remote-controlled 4-wheeled rover to compete in a timed race. The engineering focus was on creating a chassis that could handle high-speed transitions without compromising structural integrity or losing traction.

Weight Distribution

Strategically placed heavy components (batteries/motors) to achieve a low Center of Gravity, reducing body roll during cornering.

Drivetrain Efficiency

Calculated gear ratios (45:15) and used the smallest possible gear ratio to maintain a competitive top speed.

Manufacturing & Testing

The chassis and overall rover was developed using a waterfall approach. Material choice was balanced between the low weight of Coroplast and the ease of manufacturing of 3D-printed components.

Outcome

The final rover demonstrated good handling characteristics during track trials, successfully navigating the technical course without structural failure. However, traction loss was a significant issue. Despite this, our team was able to achieve a 2nd place time out of 32 teams, highlighting the effectiveness of our design approach and the potential for further optimization in future iterations.

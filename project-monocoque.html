Structural Design & Analysis

Electric Racing Monocoque Chassis

An exploration into lightweight composite structures, optimizing for torsional stiffness and driver safety in a high-performance electric racing environment.

SOLIDWORKS ANSYS FEA COMPOSITES DFM

Fig 1.0: Isometric CAD view of final monocoque assembly

Engineering Challenge

The primary objective was to transition from a traditional steel space-frame to a carbon-fiber reinforced polymer (CFRP) monocoque. The design needed to satisfy strict competition safety regulations while achieving a significant reduction in vehicle mass.

Key Metric: Stiffness

Targeted a torsional stiffness of 2,500 Nm/deg to ensure predictable suspension kinematics.

Key Metric: Mass

Reduced primary structure weight by 22% compared to previous season's tubular chassis.

Finite Element Analysis (FEA)

Using ANSYS, I conducted extensive simulations to validate the laminate schedule. This involved testing for front impact (20g), side-impact protection, and harness attachment point pull-out strength.

Project Specifications

  • Material Selection T700 Carbon Fiber / Nomex Honeycomb Core
  • Software Stack SolidWorks, ANSYS, ACP (Composite PrepPost)
  • Safety Standards FSAE / SES Structural Requirements
  • Timeline 8 Months (Concept to Validation)

Role: Lead Designer

Responsible for surface modeling, laminate schedule definition, and coordinating with the manufacturing team for the vacuum bagging process.
